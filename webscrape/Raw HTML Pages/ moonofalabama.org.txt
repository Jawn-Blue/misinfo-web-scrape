<h3>Stuck In Mud</h3>
<p>I am feeling a bit like the operator of that excavator. There is too much to do and too little motivating progress.</p>
<center><img src="https://www.moonofalabama.org/11i/suez5-s.jpg"><br/><a href="https://www.moonofalabama.org/11i/suez5.jpg">bigger</a></img></center>
<p>Those are 200,000 metric tons stuck in sand and gravel on both ends. Too much weight for the usual tugs to pull it off.</p>
<center><img src="https://www.moonofalabama.org/11i/suez2-s.jpg"/><br/><a href="https://www.moonofalabama.org/11i/suez2.jpg">bigger</a></center>
<p>They will probably have to lighten the ship and dredge the sand at both end . That may well take several days or weeks.</p>
<center><img src="https://www.moonofalabama.org/11i/suez3-s.jpg"/><br/><a href="https://www.moonofalabama.org/11i/suez3.jpg">bigger</a></center>
<p>These swimming walls are now too big. They are difficult to maneuver. With this height of the load the slightest gust of wind will move the ship in unintended ways. There <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2021/03/25/world/middleeast/suez-canal-ship.html">was a sand storm</a> in the area when this one got stuck. Unless a crew is extremely attentive and immediately uses the bow and rear thrusters any gust will push it off course. Add the <a href="https://twitter.com/bhgreeley/status/1375065063560785923">bank effect</a> which sucks the ship towards the canal walls and such an outcome is inevitable.</p>
<blockquote>Brendan Greeley @bhgreeley - <a href="https://twitter.com/bhgreeley/status/1375065063560785923">12:40 UTC · Mar 25, 2021</a>
<p>Water moves differently around a boat in a tight canal than it does in the open ocean. When water gets squeezed between the hull and the sand, the water accelerates, and its pressure drops /2<br/>When the hull gets too close to the bank, the pressure drop sucks the hull in to the bank. This is called the "bank effect." In shallow water, like in the Suez, the stern moves toward the bank, but the bow moves away. The boat spins. /3<br/>The bigger the hull, the more water it displaces, the stronger the effect. The closer the hull is to the bank, the stronger the effect. So big, wide boat = strong bank effect. And container ships are getting HUGE. /4</p>
</blockquote>
<center><img src="https://www.moonofalabama.org/11i/suez4-s.jpg"/><br/><a href="https://www.moonofalabama.org/11i/suez4.jpg">bigger</a></center>

<h3>Afghanistan - More Dead End Proposals Seek Time To Allow U.S. Face Saving Exit</h3>
<p>The Doha agreement between the U.S. and the Taliban includes a promise by the Taliban to not attack U.S. troops or major cities. In exchange the U.S. promised to leave Afghanistan by May 1. The problem for the U.S. is that leaving Afghanistan will inevitable lead to a new Taliban regime, likely within a few months. It would make the U.S. look weak. That is something that Washington inherently dislikes.</p>
<p>In early March the Biden administration launched a new Afghanistan peace initiative. It proposed to create a new interim government with participation of the Taliban and under a new constitution. The idea is to uphold some picture of normalcy that can hold for a few months while the U.S. skips out. We said that the idea was <a href="https://www.moonofalabama.org/2021/03/us-launches-a-new-afghanistan-initiative-which-is-unlikely-to-fly.html">unlikely to fly</a>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>President Ghani <a href="https://twitter.com/sayedsalahuddin/status/1368875659209150465">is furious</a> about Blinken's letter. Other interest groups in the Afghan government also <a href="https://pajhwok.com/2021/03/08/saleh-abdullah-differently-respond-to-blinken-letter/">reject</a> it. They think it is a bluff. Unless the U.S. stops the money flow to Kabul and pulls out its troops there is no need for Ghani and other to proceed.</p>
<p>The Taliban will also reject the proposals. They want the U.S. to leave and they feel sure that, after that, they can win the civil war and reinstall their Islamic Emirate. Their backers in Pakistan and Saudi Arabia are likewise convinced that there is no need to change course.</p>
<p>The new U.S. proposal is a dead end.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Today President Ashraf Ghani made <a href="https://news.yahoo.com/exclusive-rejecting-u-peace-plan-111631826.html">a counterproposal</a> which has a similar chance to be realized:</p>
<blockquote>Afghan President Ashraf Ghani will propose a new presidential election within six months, under a peace plan he will put forward as a counter-offer to a U.S. proposal that he rejects, two senior government officials told Reuters.
<p>Ghani will unveil his proposal at an international gathering in Turkey next month, signalling his refusal to accept Washington's plan for his elected government to be replaced by an interim administration, the officials said. <br>...<br>"The counterproposal which we are going to present at the Istanbul meeting would be to call for early presidential elections if the Taliban agree on a ceasefire," one senior government official said on condition of anonymity.</br></br></p>
<p>Another Afghan government official said: "The president would never agree to step aside and any future government should be formed through democratic process, not a political deal."</p>
<p>A third senior official also said Ghani's proposal would include possible early elections, although he did not specify the exact time frame for the vote. The third official said Ghani had already shared his road map with Khalilzad.<br/>...The Afghan officials said that as part of Ghani's counter-proposal, his government would ask the U.N. to closely observe the new election to ensure it is accepted by all sides.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>There is no way that a credible election could happen in Afghanistan within the next few years. If the U.S. wants to get out of Afghanistan while leaving behind some functioning government its must move Ghani out of the way. As The U.S. holds the purse without which Ghani can't do anything that should not be too hard to do.</p>
<p>Meanwhile the Taliban have offered <a href="https://tolonews.com/afghanistan-170894">their own plan</a> which would allow for the U.S. to have some time to save face:</p>

<h3>Syria - The War Is Resuming On Several Fronts</h3>
<p>Ten years after it began, and after a short lull in fighting, the war on Syria seems now to resume on several fronts.</p>
<center><img src="https://www.moonofalabama.org/11i/syriamap20210322-s.jpg"/><br><a href="https://www.moonofalabama.org/11i/syriamap20210322.jpg">bigger</a></br></center>

<blockquote>Now, as talk of democracy, freedom and human rights are in vogue anew, humanity’s actions in Syria will be the ultimate measure of our sincerity. I believe that restoring peace and stability in the region depends on genuine and strong Western support for Turkey. <br/>...<br/>Unfortunately, the moderate rebels, our local partners, have become the target of a coordinated smear campaign despite their hard work and sacrifice to defeat ISIS and the Kurdistan Workers’ Party, or PKK, another designated terrorist organization.
<p>The safe zones, which Turkey created in cooperation with its local partners, are proof of our commitment to Syria’s future. These areas have become islands of peace and stability, as well as self-sustaining ecosystems.</p>

<h3>U.S. Aggressiveness Will Accelerate Its Demise</h3>
<p>The foreign policy of the current U.S. administration is exactly the same as the foreign policy of the previous one. In short: disastrous.</p>
<p>There are dozens of examples: The "maximum pressure' campaign against Iran continues, the sanctions on Venezuela will be upheld or even strengthened, the bombing of Syria, no change on Yemen and so on.</p>
<p>The problem is that none of these 'we are tough guys' policies achieves any purpose.</p>
<p>From the outside world the behavior and tough talk of U.S. officials is seen as juvenile. It demonstrates a  lack of knowledge, wisdom and strategy.</p>
<p>Consider these recent headlines about China:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2021-02-18/quad-foreign-ministers-to-discuss-indo-pacific-covid-19">Blinken Rallies ‘Quad’ Democracies to Counter Assertive China</a> - February 17, <em>Bloomberg</em></li>
<li><a href="https://news.yahoo.com/blinken-warns-china-against-coercion-144858350.html">Blinken warns China against 'coercion and aggression'</a> - March 16, <em>Reuters</em></li>
<li><a href="https://asia.nikkei.com/Editor-s-Picks/Interview/Blinken-denounces-China-s-strings-attached-vaccine-diplomacy">Blinken denounces China's 'strings attached' vaccine diplomacy</a> - March 17, <em>Nikkei</em></li>

<li><a href="https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-china-alaska/bolstered-by-allies-biden-officials-take-blunt-message-to-first-china-talks-idUSKBN2B90FU">Bolstered by allies, Biden officials take blunt message to first China talks</a> - March 17, <em>AP</em></li>
</ul>
<blockquote>&gt; The United States will take an uncompromising stance in talks with China on Thursday in Alaska, officials have said, in the first face-to-face meetings between senior officials from the two rivals since U.S. President Joe Biden took office.
<p>Beijing has called for a reset to ties, now at their lowest in decades, but Washington has said the Alaska talks will be a one-off, and any future engagement depends on China improving its behavior. &lt;</p>
</blockquote>
<p>Then, after days of badmouthing China, it finally dawned on Blinken that he needs China's help.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://apnews.com/article/seoul-diplomacy-south-korea-north-korea-pyongyang-c9b6c751a8c94c95fe2670484c8e1c8f">Blinken urges China to convince North Korea to denuclearize</a> - March 18, <em>AP</em></li>
<li><a href="https://www.msn.com/en-gb/news/world/china-must-persuade-north-korea-to-give-up-nuclear-weapons-says-blinken/ar-BB1eIjxL">China must persuade North Korea to give up nuclear weapons, says Blinken</a> - March 18, <em>Guardian</em></li>
</ul>
<p>Why, after so many bad words towards it, would China help the U.S. with solving the North Korea problem? It has zero incentive to do so.</p>
<p>The same aggressive behavior can be seen with regards to Russia. Baseless accusations of Russian election interferences are followed with more sanctions and threats topped off with <a href="http://thesaker.is/uncle-shmuel-is-truly-brain-dead/">Biden calling Russia's President Putin a 'killer'</a>. As the Canadian Russia expert Paul Robinson <a href="https://irrussianality.wordpress.com/2021/03/17/russia-stories-rolling-up-like-london-buses/">writes</a>:</p>
<blockquote>As for Biden’s comments, well what can one say? Didn’t he just order the bombing of Syria. Doesn’t that make him a ‘killer’ too? Politicians should avoid this sort of language. I suspect, though, that what this and the intelligence report mentioned above indicate is that <strong>Russiagate</strong>, with its allegations of Trump-Putin collusion to undermine American democracy, <strong>has done irreparable damage to US-Russia relations. One gets the impression that there is now a deep, deep hatred of Russia within the US government, a hatred that <span style="text-decoration: underline;">prevents any sane analysis of Russian intentions and actions, as well as of US national interests</span>.</strong> I fear that this will last for quite a long time.</blockquote>
