<h2 class="title" itemprop="headline">Constant Success</h2>
<span class="details">
                by <span itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span class="author" itemprop="name">Remy Porter</span></span>
                in <span class="source" itemprop="articleSection">CodeSOD</span>
                on <span class="date" content="2021-03-25" itemprop="datePublished">2021-03-25</span>
</span>
<div itemprop="articleBody">
<p><strong>Dan</strong> was reviewing some PHP code written by a co-worker, as part of taking on a project. The code was in “support” mode, rarely receiving changes, getting bug fixes only when <em>absolutely</em> necessary, and nobody really wanted to be the person responsible for it.</p>
<p>One of those “not <em>absolutely</em> necessary” bugs was that sometimes, it just didn’t save data. The user would enter a product listing, hit save, get a success message back, but the listing wouldn’t actually be saved. No one had really dug into it, because having the end user do double data entry didn’t bother anyone but the end user.</p>

<h2 class="title" itemprop="headline">UI That Looks Like $900 Million Bucks</h2>
<span class="details">
                by <span itemprop="author" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><span class="author" itemprop="name">Amit Kooner</span></span>
                in <span class="source" itemprop="articleSection">News Roundup</span>
                on <span class="date" content="2021-03-22" itemprop="datePublished">2021-03-22</span>
</span>
<div itemprop="articleBody">
<p>User experience, and its related topic of user interface design, are important. How important? Well the <a href="https://www.usability.gov/what-and-why/user-interface-design.html">US government’s General Service Administration (GSA)</a> took the time to build a website to explain what it is. What other proof do we need? Well not only did the GSA build a website, but they invested in the SEO necessary to make it top of Google organic search, right below the featured snippet from <a href="http://interaction-design.org">interaction-design.org</a>.</p>
<img alt="Screenshot of Google search results, showing the US Usability site as the first hit" itemprop="image" src="/images/amit/roundup20210322/image1.png" title="User Interface Design Search"/>
<p>Which is why the saga and ongoing story of <a href="https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2021/02/citibank-just-got-a-500-million-lesson-in-the-importance-of-ui-design/">Citibank’s debt repayment blunder</a> all the more amazing. Here’s a quick recap:</p>
<style>
li { list-style: disc inside; line-height: normal }
</style>
<ul>
<li>Revlon, which owed nearly $900 million to creditors from a 2016 debt facility, was working with Citibank to refinance their current obligations into a new loan</li>
<li>Citibank, who managed loan payments, intended to send out $7.8 million of interest payments to creditors using a system called Flexcube</li>
<li>Citibank intended to roll over the remaining loan principal into an internal “wash account” to avoid sending the entire amount owed to creditors</li>
<li>Instead, the <span style="text-decoration:underline;">offshore Citibank contractor</span> responsible for this massive responsibility, misread the Flexcube UI and sent $900 million to all of Revlon’s creditors</li>
<li>Citibank begged for the cash back, and received $400 million back</li>
<li>A judge ruled that the remaining creditors can keep the cash. Citibank is miffed.</li>
<li>If you want more details written in only the way that Matt Levine at Bloomberg can, <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/opinion/articles/2021-02-17/citi-can-t-have-its-900-million-back">check out his piece from February</a>.</li>
</ul>
