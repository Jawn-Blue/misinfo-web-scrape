<div><font color="#253B6D"><b>Welcome to SCEPCOP - The Scientific Committee to Evaluate PseudoSkeptical Criticism of the Paranormal</b></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><b>SCEPCOP</b></font><font color="#253B6D"> is the 1st Coalition of Researchers, Investigators, Writers, Truth Seekers, Freethinkers and Intellectuals united to counter and expose the PseudoSkepticism movement for their fallacies, religious fanaticism, unscientific behavior, misinformation, denial, suppression, bigotry and ridicule toward all data, experience or science that challenges orthodoxy or does not fit into a materialistic paradigm.  We do not support every quack claim out there, but advocate true skepticism, proper application of the Scientific Method, objectivity and unbiased open inquiry toward all paranormal and unconventional data.  We are the world's first counter-pseudoskeptic movement providing resources, articles, books, videos and an online community.</font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">We support the proper application of the </font><font color="#253B6D"><i>Scientific Method</i></font><font color="#253B6D">, which involves the updating of one's hypothesis to the fit the data, rather than rejecting any and all data that doesn't fit into an </font><font color="#253B6D"><i>a priori</i></font><font color="#253B6D"> hypothesis, which the PseudoSkeptics do, which is totally unscientific and the antithesis of the </font><font color="#253B6D"><i>Scientific Method.</i></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><b>Background</b></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">The Paranormal is one of the most exciting frontiers today.  Research into Consciousness, Quantum Physics and Psychic Phenomena, etc. explores venues that are unlocking the mysteries of the universe and gateways to other dimensions or levels of reality and consciousness.  These provide deeper insights into a larger nature of reality, revealing insights into the meaning of existence and spawning hope for a better future in which humanity's consciousness and awareness are elevated to new heights, transcending greed, lust for power, hatred, violence, ignorance, and the confines of a materialistic paradigm that keeps us living in fear.  Therefore, the study of the Paranormal is a key stepping stone for humanity's next stage of evolution.</font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">However, standing in the way are groups of organized fundamentalists who call themselves "skeptics" but in reality know nothing about the true meaning of the word nor practice it. In fact, they've hijacked the word to mean its opposite.  Rather than </font><font color="#253B6D"><i>inquiring</i></font><font color="#253B6D">, or asking questions to try to understand something, they seek to debunk, discredit and ridicule anything that doesn't fit into their belief system.  </font></div>
<div><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrrho" title=""><br/></a></div>
<div><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrrho" title="">Pyrrho</a><font color="#253B6D">, the founder of "Skepticism", intended for it to be about </font><font color="#253B6D"><i>open inquiry and suspension of judgment.</i></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">    </font><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skeptic" title="">http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Skeptic</a></div>
<ul>
<li><font color="#000000">In classical philosophy, skepticism refers to the teachings and the traits of the 'Skeptikoi', a school of philosophers of whom it was said that they 'asserted nothing but only opined.' (Liddell and Scott) In this sense, philosophical skepticism, or Pyrrhonism, is the philosophical position that one should suspend judgment in investigations.[1]</font></li>
</ul>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">And according to </font><font color="#253B6D"><i>Webster's Revised Unabridged Dictionary</i></font><font color="#253B6D">, a skeptic is:</font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<ul>
<li><font color="#000000">"One who is yet undecided as to what is true; one who is looking or inquiring for what is true; an inquirer after facts or reasons."</font></li>
</ul>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">But rather than </font><font color="#253B6D"><i>suspending judgment</i></font><font color="#253B6D">, they attack all paranormal claims.  Hence, we call them </font><font color="#253B6D"><b><i>pseudoskeptics </i></b></font><font color="#253B6D">(a term coined by the late </font><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcello_Truzzi" title="">Marcello Truzzi</a><font color="#253B6D">)</font><font color="#253B6D"><b><i> </i></b></font><font color="#253B6D">for their actions and behaviors are the complete </font><font color="#253B6D"><i>antithesis</i></font><font color="#253B6D"> of what skepticism truly means. A "true skeptic" objectively inquires and seeks evidence, challenging all sides including their own beliefs </font><a href="characteristics.php" title="">(see here)</a><font color="#253B6D">.  But these pseudoskeptics do anything but.  This person nailed them:</font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<ul>
<li><font color="#000000">"The original definition of skeptic was a person who questions ALL beliefs, facts, and points-of-view. A healthy perspective in my opinion. Today's common definition of skeptic is someone who questions any belief that strays outside of the status quo, yet leaving the status quo itself completely unquestioned. Kind of a juvenile and intellectually lazy practice in my opinion."</font></li>
</ul>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">Similarly, this insightful YouTuber hit the bull's eye with this comment:</font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">    </font><a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vks49Bfn544" title="">http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vks49Bfn544</a></div>
<ul>
<li><font color="#000000">"What skeptics fail to understand is that skepticism involves being skeptical of your own position, it does not mean just being skeptical of that which you do not believe in, otherwise we are all skeptics and that renders their use of the term "skeptic" meaningless. A true skeptic casts skepticism on their own position as well. Since the Randi crowd do not employ skepticism in this respect then they are fairly termed pseudo skeptics and demean the term skepticism."</font></li>
</ul>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">Likewise:</font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">    </font><a href="http://www.happierabroad.com/forum/viewtopic.php?t=9196" title="">http://www.happierabroad.com/forum/viewtopic.php?t=9196</a></div>
<ul>
<li><font color="#000000">"I've never trusted skeptics, for the very reason that they are willing to accept the official version of things without a </font><font color="#000000"><b>shred of proof</b></font><font color="#000000"> but require unrealistic amounts of evidence to accept any other possibility."</font></li>
</ul>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">Therefore, in truth and by their actions, these </font><font color="#253B6D"><b>pseudoskeptics (who call themselves "skeptics") are NOT open minded truth seekers who question things and are attuned to possibilities. Rather, they are ridiculers and prosecutors of anything that strays outside the status quo or challenges the official version of things. They are defenders of orthodoxy and materialism.</b></font><font color="#253B6D"> And they will distort, dismiss, obfuscate and play "verbal hopscotch" to get their way.</font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">They've hijacked the term "skeptic" to refer to the one who </font><font color="#253B6D"><b>suppresses the act of questioning, </b></font><font color="#253B6D">rather than to the questioner himself.  In doing so, they've pretended to be the opposite of what they are to hide their true agenda, which is to protect the agenda of the status quo power elite and keep people remaining sheeple. </font><a href="hijackingterms.php" title="">See here for more info.</a></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">Additionally, they've hijacked terms such as "rational, reason, logic, critical thinking" to mean the "proper" thinking and behavior that supports materialism and orthodoxy, and rejects against anything that challenges it. That is not what those words mean of course. It's a form of mind control and disinformation. And it seems way too calculated and militant to be due to some accidental misunderstanding, ignorance or closed mindedness. Hijacking a word to mean its opposite is more indicative of a deliberate agenda, such as a </font><font color="#253B6D"><b>disinformation campaign or form of mind control</b></font><font color="#253B6D">. If that sounds terrible, well, we are here to expose it thank goodness.</font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">Furthermore, oddly enough, they treat Science as if it were some kind of authoritarian "entity" that takes positions and views on issues (their own of course), when it is in fact merely a tool and method of inquiry based on logical principles. In reality, science does not take positions or hold dogmatic beliefs on paranormal or conspiratorial subjects. People take positions, not Science, which holds no more views than my computer does. Science is not a living entity. These pseudoskeptics are projecting their own views and Atheistic philosophy into Science, which they hold as the ultimate authority, aka </font><font color="#253B6D"><i>Scientism</i></font><font color="#253B6D">. (Oh well, I guess pseudoskeptics need </font><font color="#253B6D"><i>something</i></font><font color="#253B6D"> to worship too) </font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">The pseudoskeptics have formed organizations such as </font><a href="http://www.csicop.org" title="">CSICOP</a><font color="#253B6D"> (Committee for the Scientific Investigation of Claims of the Paranormal, now known as CSI - Committee for Skeptical Inquiry), </font><a href="http://www.randi.org" title="">JREF</a><font color="#253B6D"> (James Randi Educational Foundation) professional Skeptics such as </font><a href="http://www.michaelshermer.com" title="">Michael Shermer</a><font color="#253B6D"> of </font><i><a href="http://www.skeptic.com" title="">Skeptic Magazine</a></i><font color="#253B6D">, as well as many smaller groups, blogs and online communities.  In media reports and programs on paranormal phenomena, these skeptics are often presented as the final authorities on the matter and go virtually unchallenged.  And unfortunately, most paranormal/psychic researchers have neither the time nor interest to debate these debunkers.  </font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">So now it's our turn to form a counter movement.  Enter SCEPCOP.  As CSICOP was formed to "police the claims of psi", SCEPCOP now in turn acts to "police the pseudoskeptics".  We will debunk their arguments, and reveal their fallacies, misinformation, agenda, denial, inconsistencies, pseudo-dogmas posing as "rules of logic" and double standards, showing that they are not objective truth seekers.</font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">Back in 2001, we were the first to write a book length treatise debunking </font><a href="http://www.debunkingskeptics.com/Contents.htm" title="">30 primary arguments of these pseudoskeptics</a><font color="#253B6D">.  Much needed at the time, it quickly became acclaimed in paranormal circles as a comprehensive masterpiece and first-of-its-kind.  And now he's taken it to the next step in forming the SCEPCOP coalition, a movement needed and long overdue, </font><a href="http://www.google.com/search?hl=en&amp;q=scepcop&amp;btnG=Google+Search&amp;meta=&amp;aq=f&amp;oq=" title="">evidenced by the attention that SCEPCOP received from many paranormal websites</a><font color="#253B6D">.</font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">Just to state for the record though, opposing pseudoskepticism does NOT mean that SCEPCOP endorses every crackpot claim, theory and fantasy out there. Not at all.  We believe in applying equal skepticism to both sides, as well as the objective weighing of evidence, for as you might expect, some claims and phenomenon have a lot of solid evidence to back it up, while others have little or none.</font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D"><br/></font></div>
<div><font color="#253B6D">One thing to keep in mind.  All pseudoskeptics will claim to be true skeptics, just like all high pressured salesmen claim to not be high pressure, all liars and con artists claim to be sincere, and all politicians claim to be honest.  But as you know, </font><font color="#253B6D"><b>ACTIONS speak louder than words,</b></font><font color="#253B6D"> so the proof of what they are is in their ACTIONS, not words.  If a salesman for example, told you "I am not a high pressure salesman" but then proceeds to pressure you to buy his product/service, becoming pushy and not taking no for an answer, then what do you believe, his words or his actions?  Likewise, when a skeptic tells you that he is a true skeptic who is open to evidence, yet he displays </font><a href="characteristics.php" title="">all the characteristics, traits and behaviors of a pseudoskeptic</a><font color="#253B6D">, do you listen to his words or his actions?  The answer is obvious.</font></div>
