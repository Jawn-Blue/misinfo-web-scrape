<h3><strong>Silver Yo-Yo</strong></h3>
<p>The price of silver is going up and down like a yo-yo. On Sunday and into the first part of Monday, the price skyrocketed on news that Reddit was touting the metal. <h3><strong>The Lure of Easy Money</strong></h3>
<p>Right now happens to be an attractive time to do something stupid.  Whats more, everyone is doing it.  Maybe you are too. Stock valuations and corporate earnings growth no longer appear to matter.  Why not buy an S&amp;P 500 index fund and let it ride?  Or, better yet, why not buy shares of Nvidia?</p>
<p> </p>
<p><a href="https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/12/1-NVDA-weekly.png" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-55377" height="571" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" src="https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/12/1-NVDA-weekly-1024x913.png" srcset="https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/12/1-NVDA-weekly-1024x913.png 1024w, https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/12/1-NVDA-weekly-300x268.png 300w, https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/12/1-NVDA-weekly-768x685.png 768w, https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/12/1-NVDA-weekly.png 1565w" width="640"/></a>NVDA, weekly, over the past 6 years. Other favorable narratives accompanied the rise of NVDA and other tech stocks specifically, such as the cryptocurrency mania and the increase in demand fostered by the pandemic. Add to that the biggest driver of them all  an unprecedented exercise in money printing by the Fed and other central banks  and a bubble for the ages emerged. And that is where we now are: in the greatest stock market mania of all time. [PT]</p>

<h3><strong>Prophet of Doom </strong></h3>
<p>In 1976, economist Herbert Stein, father of Ben Stein, the economics professor in <em>Ferris Buellers Day Off</em>, observed that U.S. government debt was on an unsustainable trajectory.  He, thus, established Steins Law:</p>
<p> </p>
<blockquote><p><em>If something cannot go on forever, it will stop.</em></p></blockquote>
<p> </p>

<h3><strong>Consumption without Production</strong></h3>
<p><em>Every man is a consumer, and ought to be a producer</em>, observed 19th century philosopher Ralph Waldo Emerson.  <em>He is by constitution expensive, and needs to be rich.</em></p>
<p> </p>
<p><img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-55347" height="512" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" src="https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/08/waldo.jpg" srcset="https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/08/waldo.jpg 1000w, https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/08/waldo-300x240.jpg 300w, https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/08/waldo-768x614.jpg 768w" width="640"/>Ralph Waldo Emerson (May 25, 1803  April 27, 1882), who <em>inter alia</em> opined on consumers and the need to not only consume, but also produce. The latter activity has recently become even more severely hampered than it already was. And yet, government is spending like a drunken sailor. [PT]</p>

<h3><strong>The Sage Does a 180</strong></h3>
<p>The big news in the monetary metals is that <em>Warren Buffett  famed disliker of gold  sold bank stocks to buy gold mining shares</em>. What is interesting to us is not that we think he has any special powers to predict the gold price. After all, he famously bet on silver, and lost.</p>
<p> </p>
<p><a href="https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/08/1-GOLD-daily.png" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"><img alt="" class="aligncenter wp-image-55340" height="473" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px" src="https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/08/1-GOLD-daily-1024x757.png" srcset="https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/08/1-GOLD-daily-1024x757.png 1024w, https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/08/1-GOLD-daily-300x222.png 300w, https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/08/1-GOLD-daily-768x568.png 768w, https://acting-man.com/blog/media/2020/08/1-GOLD-daily.png 1565w" width="640"/></a>GOLD, daily, over the past two years. The Sage is a bit late to the party, but his entry confirms that the sector is now entering the recognition phase  the stage at which the investoriat-at-large realizes something is afoot. [PT]</p>
